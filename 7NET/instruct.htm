Instructions

  1. Select from the Table of Contents at left to view a specific book here in the Text Window.
  2. The "NET Contents" link in the upper left-hand window will take you back to the list of books.
  3. Scroll through the text using scroll bar at right of this window.
  4. Click on footnote mark in main text and notes will appear in Notes Window below.
  5. Notes may be scrolled manually in Notes Window below.
  6. Drag on frames to resize windows.
  7. To go back in a frame, use "Back" button on your browser.
  8. The "BSF Home" link will take you to the home page of the Biblical Studies Foundation.
Recommended settings for Browser:
Be sure the cache in your browser is turned on. When you open a large book like Genesis or Luke, it will take a while to download the complete text to your computer. Likewise, when you click on your first footnote, it will take even longer to download the notes to the Notes Window (there are typically twice as much text in the notes file as in the main document). If you don't have your cache turned on, it will take a long time everytime you click on a footnote because it re-downloads the whole note file. Usually the cache is turned on, so you probably don't have to worry about it, but if each click on a footnote takes forever, that is the problem.
